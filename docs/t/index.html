Charging Modes

By using Elecq Power Monitor alongside Elecq Home, you can easily charge with solar power via our app to save on your energy bill.


Select the best charging mode based on your electricity plan: Only Solar, Solar Priority or Unlimited, to balance charging efficiency with charging cost.

Only Solar
The Elecq chargers exclusively use solar power for charging, without drawing any electricity from the grid.
Solar Priority
Prioritize using solar power for charging. If the solar output is insufficient, the charger will operate at the minimum power (6A). If there is enough solar energy, it will rely solely on that, without drawing from the grid.
Unlimited
Prioritize using solar power, with the grid supplying any shortfall. In this case, the charger will still operate at its maximum power output.

Smart

Smart Phase Switch for PV <4.2kW

When PV power to Elecq is less than 4.2 kW in total*. Elecq switches off phases 2 and 3 to single phase charging, enabling excess PV power greater than 1.4 kW to be used. Since billing with the energy supplier is only based on the cumulative power consumption from the grid over all three phases, this means no energy consumption or excess from this point of view, despite the 1-phase charging with higher currents. If the excess PV power rises above 4.2 kW again, Elecq switches back to three phases charging.

*EV require a minimum current of 6A per phase to start a charging process, which corresponds to 1.4kW in 1 phase and 4.2 kW in 3 phases.

    Pre-mount with Elecq Ready

    Elecq Ready allows you to pre-mount the backplate for easy deployment without needing an electrician during actual use. This is the perfect choice, especially since the revised Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (EPBD) came into force, requiring pre-cabling for at least 50% of parking spaces.

    The Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (EPBD)：

    • New buildings and major renovations (residential and non-residential) must include pre-cabling (installation of conduits for electrical wiring) to facilitate the future installation of EV charging points.
    • Residential buildings with more than 10 parking spaces must ensure every parking space is pre-cabled.
    • Non-residential buildings with more than 10 parking spaces must install at least one charging point and pre-cable for 20% of parking spaces.
    • From 2025, all existing non-residential buildings with more than 20 parking spaces must install at least one charging point.
    • Charging points must support smart charging capabilities, enabling dynamic energy management and integration with renewable energy sources.
    • Member States are required to incentivize the installation of EV charging points and pre-cabling during building renovations.
    *https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=OJ:L_202401275&pk_keyword=Energy&pk_content=Directive
